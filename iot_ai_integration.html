  • Fundamentals of IoT and AI Integration: Combining IoT's real-world sensing with AI's decision-making enables intelligent automation across smart homes, cities, agriculture, and more.
  • Sensors, Actuators, and Edge Devices: Sensors collect data (e.g., temperature), actuators perform actions (e.g., turn on fan), and edge devices process data locally to reduce latency.
  • IoT System Architecture & Protocols: Layered structure includes sensing, networking, and cloud layers. Protocols like MQTT, CoAP, and HTTP govern device communication.
  • Data Collection and Management: Involves acquiring, preprocessing, and storing sensor data, often in cloud or edge databases, and maintaining security and integrity.
  • IoT Analytics: Applying data analysis or AI/ML models to gain insights, make predictions, or automate actions from IoT data streams.
  • IoT Networks: Include LPWAN (e.g., LoRaWAN), Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Zigbee. Selection depends on range, power, and bandwidth needs.
  • AI Techniques for IoT: Classification, clustering, anomaly detection, reinforcement learning ‚Äî tailored for low-power execution and real-time inference.
  • Deployment on Edge Devices: Lightweight AI models (e.g., quantized neural networks) are deployed on devices like Raspberry Pi, ESP32, or microcontrollers for offline inference.
